Why This, After Excel, Power BI, Python & SQL

When the whole reporting cycle should run itself.

A finance team can spend 70% of month-end just collecting data from ERP, POS, CRM and bank statements before any actual analysis starts. Automation is the layer that removes that collection step entirely — an n8n or Power Automate workflow pulls data on a schedule, a verification layer checks it, and only then does anyone (human or Claude AI) get to look at it. No-code doesn't mean unreviewed; it means the review happens by design, every time, instead of by memory.

Platforms

The right tool for the platform you already run on

Automation should fit the systems you already use, not force a migration. Whichever of these your business already runs on, that's the one used.

Complex Workflows

n8n

Self-hostable, most flexible for multi-step finance workflows with custom logic, API calls, and AI steps built in.

Quick Connections

Zapier

Fastest way to connect two SaaS tools that already have a Zapier integration — good for simple, low-volume triggers.

Microsoft Stack

Power Automate

The natural choice when a business already lives in Excel, SharePoint, Outlook and Teams — automation without leaving the Microsoft ecosystem.

Google Stack

Google Apps Script

Code-level automation inside Sheets, Gmail and Drive — auto-refreshing trackers, scheduled emails, custom functions Sheets can't do natively.

Mobile Apps

AppSheet

Turns a Google Sheet or Excel table into a real mobile app — field data entry, approvals, expense capture, without writing an app from scratch.

Visual Workflows

Make (Integromat)

Visual, branching automation scenarios — a strong alternative to n8n when a team prefers a fully hosted, drag-and-drop builder.
